Top 5 Beginner 3D Printers (2025)

Our expert picks for the best entry-level 3D printers — ranked and reviewed.

#1 — Bambu Lab A1

  • 500 mm/s print speed with active flow rate compensation
  • Multi-color printing via AMS Lite (up to 4 colors)
  • Auto bed leveling, Z-offset tuning, and spaghetti failure detection
  • Touchscreen + Wi-Fi cloud printing via Bambu Studio and mobile app
  • Plug-and-play setup with smart calibration — no tinkering needed
  • Eco-friendly, quiet, and beginner-proof
  • “Closest thing to plug-and-play perfection” — Maker’s Muse

#2 — Creality Ender 3 V3 SE

  • 250 mm/s print speed with dual Z-axis stability
  • Auto bed leveling with CR Touch sensor
  • Mod-friendly for upgrades and tinkering
  • Compact design and easy assembly
  • Under £200 — budget beast
  • “A gateway to the expansive world of 3D printing” — GamingTrend

#3 — Anycubic Kobra 2

  • 250 mm/s speed with LeviQ 2.0 auto leveling
  • 60W hotend handles PLA, TPU, PETG, ABS
  • 4.3" touchscreen and filament sensor
  • Power-loss recovery and PEI-coated bed
  • “Fast, affordable, and easy to assemble” — PCMag

#4 — Prusa Mini+

  • Professional-grade print quality
  • Auto mesh bed leveling and sensorless homing
  • Full-color LCD and magnetic print bed
  • Strong community + excellent support
  • “More capable and reliable than most entry-level printers” — Tom’s Guide

#5 — Elegoo Neptune 4

  • 500 mm/s max speed (recommended 250 mm/s)
  • Klipper firmware for advanced control
  • Four-fan cooling system and 121-point auto leveling
  • Touchscreen interface and direct drive extruder
  • “A true game-changer in the field” — 3DWithUs
